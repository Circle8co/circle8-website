The Philosophy

The Number at the Heart of Circle8 Now Has a Room

The best conversations in this industry don't happen in conference rooms with 40 people and a microphone. They happen when the right eight people sit down together with a real problem, a shared interest, or a single well-chosen question.

The Circle8 Round Table is built on that belief. Eight participants. Deliberately selected. Structured conversation. No audience. No slides. No selling.

The format is small by design. Eight people means everyone contributes. Eight voices means every perspective is heard. The constraint is the quality.

The Format

The Rules of 8

8 Seats Always exactly eight participants. The limit is the format.
8 Industries Developer, architect, builder, financier, designer, supplier, consultant, buyer — the full ecosystem in one room where possible.
8 Questions Eight discussion questions prepared in advance and shared with participants beforehand. Everyone arrives ready.
8 Weeks Sessions held approximately every eight weeks — frequent enough to build continuity, spaced enough to keep each one an event.

Three Session Formats

Choose Your Table

Each Round Table runs in one of three formats, announced in advance so participants know what they're walking into.

Format 01

Live Project

A developer brings a real project brief to the table. Eight industry professionals — architect, builder, financier, consultant, supplier and others — spend two hours working through it. The developer gets genuine insight from the people who will actually build, fund, and sell the project. Participants get access to a problem-solving environment that doesn't exist anywhere else.

The project can be at any stage — acquisition, feasibility, design, procurement, or pre-sale. The earlier, the better.

Best for: Developers seeking strategic input · Industry partners seeking to contribute to real projects

Format 02

Design Series

Built directly around an edition of the Circle8 Global Design Series. Paulette presents the key findings — a waterfront precinct in Istanbul, a 1,500-year-old building in Türkiye — and eight people discuss what it means for Australian development right now.

The Global Design Series becomes the curriculum. The Round Table becomes the classroom — except everyone at the table is already an expert.

Best for: Design and development professionals · Anyone who read the edition and wants to go deeper

Format 03

Open Intelligence

No project. No editorial. Just a single question posed to eight people who think about this industry every day. Announced two weeks in advance so participants can arrive with a considered point of view.

Past questions might include: "Where does trust break down in a project team?" or "What does the next five years of residential development in Sydney actually look like?"

Best for: Senior industry professionals · Those who want unfiltered peer conversation without an agenda

Why It Works

For Circle8, the Round Table Is the Business

Circle8 was built on the belief that great projects begin with the right relationships. The Round Table is that belief made tangible. It is a room where those relationships form — not transactionally, not over a networking event canape, but through shared thinking on real problems.

Every session generates content that flows back into the Global Design Series. Every participant becomes part of the Circle8 network. Every conversation creates the conditions for the next project introduction.

That is the flywheel. Editorial creates insight. Round Tables create relationships. Relationships create projects. Projects create more to write about.

What Participants Take Away

Access to Peers

Genuine peer conversation with people at the same level — not a conference, not a panel, not a pitch.

Real Insight

Structured discussion means participants leave with something they didn't have when they walked in.

The Right Introductions

Paulette curates the eight seats. The people in the room are selected for a reason.

Visibility

Insights from each session (anonymised where appropriate) contribute to the Global Design Series and Circle8's LinkedIn editorial — amplifying participants' thinking to a wider audience.
