The Series

Design That Travels. Insight That Stays.

The Global Design Series began with a simple conviction: the most interesting ideas in architecture, placemaking and urban design are happening in places most people in the Australian industry will never visit. Paulette visits them instead — and writes about what she finds.

Each edition focuses on a single building, precinct or place. Not a travel review — a considered analysis of what makes it work, why it was built the way it was, and what the Australian development industry can take from it.

Published without a schedule. Written when there is something worth saying. Read by the people who build this country's cities.

What Each Edition Covers

Written for the Industry. Grounded in the Field.

01 Place A single building, precinct or public space — explored in depth, not in passing. Every edition goes somewhere specific with a reason to be there.
02 Design First-hand observation of what was built, how it was built, and the decisions that shaped it — from materiality and scale to civic intent and commercial reality.
03 Relevance Every edition ends with the question that matters most: what does this mean for the way we design and develop in Australia right now?
